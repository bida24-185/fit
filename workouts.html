Squats

Begin in a standing positon. Engage the abdominal muscles, bend from the hips and lower the hips towards the floor. Extend both arms in front of the body with palms facing down. Raise your heels off the floor, hold for 1-2 seconds before lowering the heels back down to the floor and rising up standing position.

Intermediate Exercises

Bar pull up

Grip an overhead bar with hands shoulder-width apart (palms facing away), hang with arms fully extended, then engage your back and arms to pull your chin above the bar, and lower back down with control. (Targets lats, biceps, and upper back for upper-body strength.)

890K Reads 178 Comments

Side Bridges

Lie on your side with legs straight, prop yourself up on your forearm (elbow under shoulder), lift your hips to form a straight line from head to feet, and hold while engaging your core and glutes. (Targets obliques, shoulders, and hip stability.)

610K Reads 112 Comments

Glute Bridge

Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat, arms at your sides, then drive through your heels to lift your hips toward the ceiling until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees, squeezing your glutes at the top. Lower back down with control. (Targets glutes, hamstrings, and core while improving hip stability.)

By Trainer James

530K Reads 97 Comments

Bicycle Crunches

Lie on your back with hands behind your head (elbows wide), lift your shoulder blades off the floor, and alternate bringing one knee toward your chest while rotating your torso to touch the opposite elbow to knee (like pedaling a bike). Keep the motion controlled and engage your core throughout. (Targets obliques, rectus abdominis, and hip flexors for a dynamic ab burn!)

670K Reads 134 Comments

wide arm push ups

Place your hands wider than shoulder-width apart on the floor, keep your body straight from head to heels, then lower your chest toward the ground by bending your elbows outward (about 45 degrees). Push back up to the starting position while engaging your chest and core. (Targets the pectoralis major, shoulders, and triceps with extra emphasis on the outer chest!)

750K Reads 156 Comments

Lunges

Stand tall with feet hip-width apart, step one leg forward and lower your hips until both knees bend at 90-degree angles (front knee over ankle, back knee hovering just above the floor). Push through your front heel to return to standing, then alternate legs. (Targets quads, glutes, hamstrings, and core while improving balance and lower-body strength!)

By Coach Amanda

EXPERT LEVEL

Shoulder tap pike pushup

This movement starts from a piked push-up position, with hips high.Maintain a tight core as you lower yourself toward the floor. Aim with your forehead.Push back up, then touch your right hand to your left shoulder then alternate, touching left hand to right shoulder before doing another pushup. Repeat. Remember to keep knees locked, your shoulders level, and your core tight throughout the entire exercise.

Bridge reach overs

ADVANCED

Triceps Toe Taps

Starting position for this exercise is feet hip-width apart and arms shoulder width apart.Hold your hips off the floor. Raise your right foot as high as you can and touch the toe with your left hand. Now alternate to the other side, raising the left foot and touching the toe with the right hand.This works your internal and external obliques as you twist to the opposite side to touch your toe. You also get some nice isometric work on your triceps in the process, and of course the hip flexors and glute muscles get involved.

COMPETITIVE

Devils and Angels

Lie in a prone position on the floor, facing forward, holding your feet and shoulders off the floor. Swing your straight arms in a wide arc up over your head, touch your fingertips, and then bring your arms back down behind you as far as you can. Try to touch your thumbs together behind your back before bringing your arms overhead again.Repeat this motion keeping your shoulders and feet elevated.
